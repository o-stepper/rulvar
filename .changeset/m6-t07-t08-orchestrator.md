M6-T07 and M6-T08: the mode (c) dynamic orchestrator. `orchestrate(engine, goal, { model?, profiles?, maxSpawns?, budget?, limits? })` and `ctx.orchestrate(goal, opts)` share one implementation: an ordinary workflow whose agent (role `orchestrate`) holds the typed toolset with the normative docs/07 schemas: `spawn_agent`, `parallel_agents`, `await_any`, `await_all`, `cancel_agent`, and the loop-terminal `finish` (a new engine interception alongside escalate). Every spawn is an ordinary kind `agent` entry under the orchestrator's `agent:<seq>` scope, admitted through the single AdmissionController with the verdict, evaluated reserve, and statsBefore embedded in a `spawn-admission` decision entry (the budget debit itself rides the child's dispatch: one debit, never two); rejections surface as typed tool errors and never kill the run. Handles ARE the child dispatch seqs and stay stable across resume: a crashed orchestrator restores its transcript from the mandatory turn-boundary checkpoint, rebuilds its spawn records from the journal, redispatches only what was in flight, and finds settled children by content keys with zero re-paid spawns and no duplicate spawn decisions. `await_any`/`await_all` deliver deterministic TaskDigests; `cancel_agent` aborts an in-flight child to a `cancelled` terminal (caller intent; abandon coverage arrives with M7 cancel_task). The nested surface rides ctx.workflow, so maxDepth and the budget account tree clamp it for free; the orchestrator gets its own budget sub-account when a cap resolves (reserve decisions and the at-cap freeze are M7, DEF-7).
